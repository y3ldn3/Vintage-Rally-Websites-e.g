What Does A Co-Pilot Do?


In all of motoring, a co-driver is the most vital navigation system. It's impossible to memorize every turn of every route in the rally, unlike racing circuits, when drivers memorize every corner and repeat the bends lap after lap. However, the stakes are significantly bigger than in road racing. Your car could be sliding down a mountainside if you slide on one turn. A fall like this might cause irreversible harm, not to mention the possibility of injuries. So, in the passenger seat, a co-driver directs the driver during the rally. The co-driver makes notes at each stage and informs the driver about the corner's direction, severity, and any straightaways in between bends..


Can Fans Attend Rallys


Yes! Fortunately, fans can attend World Rally Championship events to get up close and personal with the vehicles. Seeing people lining the track at what appears to be a perilous distance is not uncommon. Tickets that guarantee entrance to specific stages of an event can be purchased. You'll watch one stage before joining the rally for the next one. This is usually a short walk or drive away, and following the pack on the road makes for an interesting trip. Hospitality packages are also available, which include entrance to a lounge where you can meet other WRC fans.
